Bennu Mission Phases (end to beginning) covered in SBIB so far

  • Sample Collection - various Opnav images; MapCam & SamCam
  • Recon C - Nightingale, various Opnav images.
  • Recon B - Nightingale, Osprey, various Opnav images.
  • Orbit R - 2019-10-28 to 2020-01-06.
  • Recon A - Nightingale, Kingfisher, Osprey, Sandpiper, various Opnav images.
  • Orbit C - 2019-08-06 to 2019-08-09.
  • Orbit B - 2019-06-08 to 2019-08-05; particle monitoring June 25,26,28, and July 1.
  • Detailed Survey - Baseball Diamond 0 to 7 (BBD-0 to BBD-7); various OpNav Images (BBD-OpNav);
                                 Equatorial Stations at several local solar times: 3:00pm (Eq-3:00pm), 3:20am (Eq-3:20am), 12:30pm (Eq-12:30pm, 10:00am (Eq-10:00am), and various OpNav images (Eq-OpNav);
                                 BBD-2 refly (BBD-2-refly), various refly OpNav images (BBD-OpNav-refly)
  • Orbit A - 2019-02-18 to 2019-02-21, MapCam only.
  • Preliminary Survey - North Pole 1, inbound (NP1-inbound); North Pole 1, outbound (NP1-outbound); North Pole 3, inbound (NP3-inbound); North Pole 3, outbound (NP3-outbound);
                                       Equatorial, inbound (Eq-inbound); Equatorial, outbound (Eq-outbound); South Pole, inbound (SP-inbound); South Pole, outbound (SP-outbound); various OpNav images (OpNAV).
  • Approach - 2018-10-18 to 2018-12-02.

Data Download

Highlight the data you would like to download, click 'Download'. Notes for the download screen: 'raw' and 'calibrated' buttons refer to the fits files. For downloading e.g. jpg files in camera view, click on 'jpg'; for projected view, click on 'Projected' and then again on 'jpg'. Note that the file names are the same for the camera and projected view.

Map Projection and ArcGIS

Data can be imported into ArcGIS by downloading the ISIS Map cubes.
Most images (PNG and thumbnails) were generated with an equirectangular projection. Images that are near the poles have been projected with a polar projection.

Data processing

We used the USGS software tool Integrated Software for Imagers and Spectrometers (ISIS) as the main tool to process the data. We used the pre-Dawn shape model posted at USGS and the ISIS software routines to convert images into map-based projections.

Coordinates

This software uses the coordinate system defined by the OSIRIS-REx mission( details).
  • Spin Pole of Bennu at RA=85.46 deg, Dec=-60.36 deg
  • Prime Meridian Angle W0=135.8156 and rotation rate W1=2011.14645095 deg/day
  • Rotation period 0.179 days
